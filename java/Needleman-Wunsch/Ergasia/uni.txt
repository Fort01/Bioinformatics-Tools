CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of a heterodimeric G-protein coupled receptor
CC       for GABA, formed by GABBR1 and GABBR2. Within the heterodimeric
CC       GABA receptor, only GABBR1 seems to bind agonists, while GABBR2
CC       mediates coupling to G proteins. Ligand binding causes a
CC       conformation change that triggers signaling via guanine
CC       nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) and modulates the
CC       activity of down-stream effectors, such as adenylate cyclase.
CC       Signaling inhibits adenylate cyclase, stimulates phospholipase A2,
CC       activates potassium channels, inactivates voltage-dependent
CC       calcium-channels and modulates inositol phospholipid hydrolysis.
CC       Calcium is required for high affinity binding to GABA. Plays a
CC       critical role in the fine-tuning of inhibitory synaptic
CC       transmission. Pre-synaptic GABA receptor inhibits neurotransmitter
CC       release by down-regulating high-voltage activated calcium
CC       channels, whereas postsynaptic GABA receptor decreases neuronal
CC       excitability by activating a prominent inwardly rectifying
CC       potassium (Kir) conductance that underlies the late inhibitory
CC       postsynaptic potentials. Not only implicated in synaptic
CC       inhibition but also in hippocampal long-term potentiation, slow
CC       wave sleep, muscle relaxation and antinociception.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9069281}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer of GABBR1 and GABBR2. Homodimers may form,
CC       but are inactive. Interacts with the leucine zipper of the C-
CC       terminal bZIP domain of ATF4 via its C-terminal region. Interacts
CC       with JAKMIP1. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10075644,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10924501, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14718537,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9872744}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC       Cell junction, synapse, postsynaptic cell membrane; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein. Cell projection, dendrite. Note=Coexpression of
CC       GABBR1 and GABBR2 is required for GABBR1 maturation and transport
CC       to the plasma membrane. Colocalizes with ATF4 in hippocampal
CC       neuron dendritic membranes.
